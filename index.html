ABOUT

Designing reliable communication systems, from classical channels to quantum codes.

I am a Research Associate working on wireless communications, information theory, channel coding, and quantum error-correcting codes. My research lies at the intersection of theory and practice—using information-theoretic principles to design efficient, robust systems.

Previously, I was a PhD student at Bilkent University under the supervision of Prof. Erdal Arıkan. My doctoral work focused on polar-like codes and applications of information theory. I also gained hands-on experience with fading and multipath channel modeling, QAM and OFDM modulation, and TDMA, OFDMA, and NOMA multiple-access schemes.

I have developed simulation and analysis pipelines for MIMO systems and spectrum-sharing scenarios, and worked on learning-based beamforming and channel estimation. My expertise includes 5G physical-layer technologies, interference control, signal and channel design, and advanced algorithm design.

FEATURED RESULT

Bounded-complexity sequential decoding for PAC codes

Sequential decoding has constant complexity when the data allocation within polarized subchannels remains below their cutoff-rate thresholds. Channel polarization raises the average cutoff rate while preserving capacity, making it possible to communicate closer to capacity without exponential decoding complexity.

Our numerical results show that short- and medium-length PAC codes approach theoretical error-correction bounds while maintaining bounded complexity.
